Chicken Biryani

Description:

Chicken Biryani is a popular and flavorful Indian dish that combines aromatic basmati rice, succulent chicken, and a blend of rich spices. It is a one-pot dish known for its fragrant and mouthwatering taste. The dish has its roots in South Asian cuisine and is widely loved for its unique combination of tender meat, fragrant rice, and a medley of spices that create a symphony of flavors. The dish often includes ingredients like saffron, fried onions, and fresh herbs, adding layers of complexity to the overall experience.

This One-Pot wonder is renowned for its rich flavors, and is sure to brighten your day all year around

Ingredients:

  • 2 Cups basmati rice
  • 500g Chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 Cup yogurt
  • 2 Large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 Tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/4 Cup cooking oil or ghee
  • 2 Tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • Whole spices (cinnamon, cardamom, cloves)
  • 1 Teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 Teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 Teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 Teaspoon biryani masala
  • A pinch of saffron strands soaked in warm milk
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the basmati rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 30 minutes. Drain the water before cooking.
  2. In a large pot, heat oil or ghee. Add cumin seeds and whole spices until they release their aroma.
  3. Add sliced onions and saut√© until golden brown. Remove a portion for garnishing.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until the raw smell disappears. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they are soft.
  5. Add chicken pieces and cook until they are browned. Add yogurt, turmeric powder, red chili powder, biryani masala, and salt. Cook until the chicken is tender.
  6. In a separate pot, bring water to a boil. Add salt and cook the soaked rice until it is 70% cooked. Drain the water.
  7. Layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken mixture. Sprinkle saffron-infused milk, fried onions, and chopped mint and coriander leaves.
  8. Cover the pot tightly with a lid or aluminum foil. Cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes or until the rice is fully cooked and aromatic.
  9. Gently mix the layers before serving, ensuring that the chicken and rice are well blended.
  10. Garnish with more fried onions, mint, and coriander leaves. Serve hot with raita or a side salad.
